Prpl

Overview

Prpl is a native iOS app that allows users to listen to their favorite artists or songs. Users can share music with their friends as well as see what other users are listening to. With Prpl, users can listen to music of their choice, search the vast collection of songs, and expand their personal libraries. Users can watch live lyrics on a screen to better learn their favorite songs or gain familiarity with new music.

I was the UI/UX designer on this project. I was the UI/UX designer in this project. I developed the initial concept, research, wireframes, style guide and mockups of the final designs. I worked on this project from early July to mid September of 2020.

Research

I identified the competitors and analyzed their applications to determine their strengths and weaknesses relative to prpl. Additionally, I found features that would be beneficial to include.

Wireframes

Low fidelity wireframes were created to ensure all necessary application features were included and accessible to all users.

Style Guide

The style choices I made followed the trends of other popular music applications. I chose a vibrant bold color to be the focal point of Prpl’s branding. Prpl creates a sense of luxury and reflects creativity while preserving a sense of simplicity. The typography and icons I selected were modern and minimalistic to appeal to my target demographic, being young adults and teens. 

Final Design & Mockups

Prpl was developed to improve the users' experience while listening to music. Vibrant displays, and exciting features were created to give the user an enjoyable experience while connecting with friends and their favorite artists. The application was created with mobile devices in mind, so users can get an optimal viewing experience on any mobile device, tablet, laptop, or desktop computer.

Conclusion

My goal was to offer users a stylish and functional way to listen, share, and save music. Through creating user stories and flows, sketching wireframes, conducting user tests, designing a style guide, and developing multiple design iterations I was able to create a mobile application that I wish I could find today on the market.
